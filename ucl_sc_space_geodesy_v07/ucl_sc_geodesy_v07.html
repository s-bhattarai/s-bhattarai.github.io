Earth-centred Coordinate Systems

1. To label points on in 3D space we need a coordinate system
2. X, Y and Z axes with origin point at (x=0,y=0,z=0)
3. The origin should be at the Earth's centre of mass. But where is that?
4. Can we assume that the center of mass and the center of geometry are the same? What geometrical object best represents Earth's shape?
5. Mostly we are interested in where things are in relation to the Earth's surface.
6. Equation of a spherical surface: $$x^2+y^2+z^2=r^2$$
7. The Earth is not spherical. Geodesists model geometric shape of the Earth as oblate spheroid (or ellipsoid).
8. General equation for an ellipsoid: $$ \frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}+\frac{z^2}{c^2}=1 $$
