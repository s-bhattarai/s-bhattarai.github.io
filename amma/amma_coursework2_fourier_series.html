Late submissions will be penalised.* # General definition for an arbitrary period $T$ The function, $f(t)$, can be expressed by its Fourier Series, $F(t)$ , at those points where $f(t)$ is continuous, if: - it is a periodic function such that $f(t+T)=f(t)$, where $T$ is the period - it defined and it is single-valued - it is piecewise continuous (i.e. there are a finite number of finite discontinuities) over a single period If these properties, the **Dirichlet conditions**, are satisfied, the Fourier Series of the function is: \begin{equation} f(t) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty{a_n\cos(n \omega t)} + \sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty{b_n\sin(n \omega t)} \end{equation} where $\omega = \frac{2 \pi}{T}$ and the coefficients of $F(t)$ are: \begin{equation} a_0 = \frac{2}{T}\int_{0}^{T}{f(t)dt} \end{equation} \begin{equation} a_n = \frac{2}{T}\int_{0}^{T}{f(t)\cos(n \omega t)dt} \end{equation} \begin{equation} b_n = \frac{2}{T}\int_{0}^{T}{f(t)\sin(n \omega t)dt} \end{equation} # Question Consider the number of people onsite at UCL Here East at any given time, $t$, to be a periodic variable, $f(t+T) = f(t)$, where the period, $T$, is 24 hours. The site is closed from 2200 to 1000 when there are only two members of security staff onsite. The doors open at 1000 at which point 12 eager students run straight into the building to work on their projects for 6 hours. Assume no one else goes in or out of the building during this time period. At 1600 some of the students begin to get tired and start leaving for home at a rate of 2 per hour until 2200, by which point all students have left and the security staff lock the doors, remaining inside. - Sketch $f(t)$ over two full cycles. - Write out the analytical expression for $f(t)$. - Find the coefficients $a_0$, $a_n$ and $b_n$ of the Fourier Series of $f(t)$. - Discuss the limitations of the model proposed in the question. In particular, consider our assumption that the number of people onsite at Here East can be represented by a variable with a repeat period of 24 hours and propose an enhanced model.
