**BARC0130 AMMA: Fourier Series I** Worksheet 2: Investigating the properties of Fourier Series # Basic Definitions (special case where period = $2\pi$) The function, $f(x)$, can be expressed by its Fourier Series, $F(x)$, at those points where $f(x)$ is continuous, if: - it is a periodic function such that $f(x+2\pi)=f(x)$, where $2\pi$ is the period - it defined and it is single-valued - it is piecewise continuous (i.e. there are a finite number of finite discontinuities) over a single period If these properties, the **Dirichlet conditions**, are satisfied, the Fourier Series of $f(x)$ is: \begin{equation} F(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty{a_n\cos(nx)} + \sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty{b_n\sin(nx)} \end{equation} where the coefficients of $F(x)$ are: \begin{equation} a_0 = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{0}^{2\pi}{f(x)dx} \end{equation} \begin{equation} a_n = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{0}^{2\pi}{f(x)\cos(nx)dx} \end{equation} \begin{equation} b_n = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{0}^{2\pi}{f(x)\sin(nx)dx} \end{equation} # Question Write out the analytical expression of the function depicted in Figure [fr_sawtooth].
